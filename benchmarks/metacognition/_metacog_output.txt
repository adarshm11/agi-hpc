======================================================================
METHODOLOGY & INTERPRETATION
======================================================================

  TEST TYPES:
    M1 (Calibration): HEADLINE TEST. Do confidence ratings predict
      accuracy? Expected Calibration Error (ECE) with 5 bins.
      Also measures overconfidence rate (conf>8 but wrong).
      Fisher combination of ECE z-scores across models.
    M2 (Knowing What You Don't Know): Does the model report lower
      confidence on genuinely ambiguous scenarios (ESH/NAH) vs
      clear-cut cases (NTA/YTA with high community agreement)?
      Uses AUROC (rank-based) alongside raw confidence gap.
      AUROC is robust when confidence is compressed into 8-10 range.
    M3 (Self-Monitoring): Can the model identify which scenarios
      it is least certain about? 12 scenarios x 5 reps each,
      then model ranks its least-certain scenarios. Spearman
      correlation between self-report and actual verdict variance.
    M4 (Strategy Selection): Does reasoning effort scale with
      scenario complexity? UNCONSTRAINED prompt (no length cap).
      v2 had '2-3 sentences MAX' which contradicted the measurement.
      Models now free to vary reasoning length naturally.

  STATISTICAL CONTROLS:
    M1-M2 include 5-rep control arms: the model re-judges
    identical text to estimate stochastic confidence baseline.
    M1 ECE significance via bootstrap standard error.
    M2 discrimination via two-sample t-test + AUROC.
    All rates reported with Wilson 95% confidence intervals.

  CROSS-FAMILY DESIGN:
    Claude Sonnet runs FULL M1-M4 suite (not just M1 probes).
    Enables genuine cross-family comparison on all 4 tests.

  THREE-TIER DATA ARCHITECTURE:
    Gold tier: hand-audited scenarios with known verdicts and
    expected confidence ranges. Highest interpretive confidence.
    Probe tier: synthetic easy/ambiguous scenarios.
    AITA tier: crowd-labeled scenarios from Reddit.

  KNOWN LIMITATIONS:
    1. Small samples: 12-52 scenarios per test. Directional evidence.
    2. AITA crowd labels are not ground truth; minority verdicts
       (ESH, NAH) have lower inter-rater agreement.
    3. M3 self-monitoring relies on a single ranking query;
       coarse measure of metacognitive access.
    4. M4 reasoning length is a proxy for reasoning effort.
    5. No temperature control; adaptive concurrency may add noise.
    6. Control arms (5 reps) provide a stochasticity
       floor but not a full variance model.
