Figure 1. Invariance violations and calibration deficit across five cognitive domains

Figure 1. Fisher-combined significance across five cognitive domains. Blue bars: pure invariance violations (morally irrelevant perturbations producing displacement beyond stochastic controls). Amber: correction susceptibility (conflates legitimate and illegitimate belief updating). Teal: metacognitive calibration deficit (internal property, not a perturbation). Dashed red line: conventional 5s discovery threshold. The Fisher combination assumes independence across models; shared scenario sets may make these values somewhat anti-conservative (see Methods).

Figure 2. The sycophancy gradient

Figure 2. The sycophancy gradient across four models. (a) Wrong-correction flip rate with Wilson 95% confidence intervals. All cells are n = 9; the wide CIs reflect small denominators. The monotonic ordering across four models is more robust than any single point estimate. (b) Confidence t-statistic in response to wrong corrections, revealing the behavioral signature underlying each model's discrimination profile.

Figure 3. Partially dissociable robustness profiles

Figure 3. Cross-track robustness profiles for four models across five selected measures. No model dominates all dimensions. Claude leads on correction discrimination (L2) but shows the worst divided attention (A4) and anchoring recovery (E2). Pro has the best counterfactual sensitivity (E3) and divided attention but moderate correction discrimination. These dissociations suggest partially independent robustness dimensions that resist collapse to a single score.

Figure 4. Preserved vs. broken symmetries

Figure 4. Selective symmetry: preserved vs. broken invariances averaged across all 5 models. Gender swap and evaluation order produce no displacement beyond stochastic baselines (high scores). Linguistic framing, emotional anchoring, and irrelevant sensory detail all produce significant displacement (lower scores). The common thread in the broken invariances is salience manipulation — making morally irrelevant features perceptually prominent.
