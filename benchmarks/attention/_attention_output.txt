======================================================================
METHODOLOGY & INTERPRETATION
======================================================================

  TEST TYPES:
    A1 (Distractor Resistance): HEADLINE TEST. Does vivid but morally
      irrelevant sensory detail (weather, food, clothing) shift severity
      ratings or flip verdicts beyond stochastic baseline?
      GRADED: Tests both VIVID (dramatic sensory) and MILD (mundane)
      distractors. Dose-response: good models show vivid > mild > control.
      Recovery via explicit warning ('ignore irrelevant details').
    A2 (Length Robustness): Does neutral filler (dates, locations, mundane
      logistics) at 2x and 4x length change moral judgment?
    A3 (Selective Attention): Do declared-relevant moral dimensions
      receive higher scores than irrelevant dimensions? Signal-to-noise
      ratio on 7-dimension scoring with hand-labeled relevance ground truth.
    A4 (Divided Attention): Does judging two scenarios in a single prompt
      change verdicts compared to judging them individually?
      Scoring relative to control (not just raw flip rate).

  STATISTICAL CONTROLS:
    A1-A4 include 5-rep control arms: the model re-judges
    identical text to estimate stochastic baseline flip rate.
    Significance via two-proportion z-test (A1 verdict, A2, A4) or
    paired t-test (A1 severity) AGAINST the control, not against zero.
    All rates reported with Wilson 95% confidence intervals.

  SEPARATION OF CONCERNS:
    A1 distractor and A2 padding text is generated by a FIXED model
    (google/gemini-2.0-flash). Models under test ONLY judge the
    pre-generated text. This eliminates the self-confirming loop.

  CROSS-FAMILY DESIGN:
    Claude Sonnet runs FULL A1-A4 suite (not just A1 probes).
    Enables genuine cross-family comparison on all 4 tests.

  THREE-TIER DATA ARCHITECTURE:
    Gold tier: hand-audited scenarios with human-written distractors.
    Probe tier: synthetic scenarios engineered for maximum control.
    Generated tier: Dear Abby scenarios with LLM-generated transforms.

  KNOWN LIMITATIONS:
    1. Small samples: 6-21 scenarios per test. Directional evidence.
    2. Distractor text generation quality varies.
    3. A4 interleaved parsing relies on structured output format.
    4. No temperature control; adaptive concurrency may add noise.
    5. Control arms (5 reps) provide a stochasticity
       floor but not a full variance model.
    6. Mild distractors only available for generated tier (9 scenarios).
       Gold and probe tiers only have vivid distractors.
