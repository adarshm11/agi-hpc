Logo #8 — Refinement Round

Six variants of the Apple & Sphere concept, varying on three axes: how the two marks connect (tether style), their proportion, and how near/far they sit. Plus standalone marks for Atlas and ErisML used separately, wordmark pairings, and a proper favicon study.

Review note. Each variant shows dark preview on the left, light preview on the right. My pick is flagged. Underneath each pair, the note says what the variant emphasizes and where it might fail.

Variants — how the two marks relate

V1 · Dashed Tether
from gallery

What it says: two products, tethered. The dash implies a live connection.

Risk: dashes are fragile at small sizes; "tether" feels more operational than architectural.

V2 · Apple as Orbiting Moon
kinetic

What it says: ErisML orbits Atlas — it's a satellite capability, not a peer product. Matches the technical reality (ErisML is a dependency).

Risk: reads more as a cosmology diagram than a logo. The orbit ring adds complexity that doesn't survive 32px.

V3 · Kissing Tangent
my pick

What it says: no artificial tether; the apple just meets the sphere at one point. Mathematical elegance — tangency is a Greek-geometry idea. The two objects are simply in contact.

Strength: cleaner, reads as unified, survives at small sizes, and the lack of a dashed line feels less "UI-chart" and more "coat of arms".

V4 · Apple Within
concentric

What it says: the apple (ErisML / safety) is at the core of Atlas. Ethics inside the sphere. Strong statement.

Risk: might misread as "apple in a fishbowl" — the sphere visually contains rather than coexists. Also loses the ecosystem duality.

V5 · Equal Peers
symmetric

What it says: Atlas and ErisML as equal products in a portfolio. Good for a company with a "two pillars" pitch.

Risk: if Atlas is really the flagship, this oversells the apple. Feels more "partnership mark" than "product mark".

V6 · Crowned Sphere
hierarchical

What it says: the apple (ethics / safety) governs Atlas from above — a literal crown. Strong alignment statement: "safety sits above the system".

Risk: asymmetric silhouette (tall, narrow) makes it harder to use in headers designed for horizontal marks.

V3 scale study — does it survive at small sizes?

V3 Kissing Tangent
200 px (header)
96 px (nav bar)
48 px (inline)
32 px (favicon)
16 px (tab)

At 16 px, the apple's stem and leaf vanish and the two marks simplify to "ring + dot". That's actually the right reduction — at favicon sizes you want a silhouette, not detail. The 48 px version drops the leaf but keeps the stem; above that, the full mark reads.
